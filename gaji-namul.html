Gaji-Namul

Gaji-Namul is a dish I usually prepair at the same time as my silken tofu for an easy prepped pairing that stays good in the fridge for up to 4 days to add to rice whenevers. This spicy steamed asian eggplant garlic and sesame explosion is wonderful hot or cold.

If you don't have a stove top steamer you can microwave your cuts of eggplant for 5-6 minutes for viable results.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of Korean eggplants (3-4 eggplants), caps removed and washed

Sauce

  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon fish sauce
  • 1 teaspoon Korean hot pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoonÂ toasted sesame seeds, crushed

Prep and Assembly

  1. Prep your steamer by adding 2 cups of water to it. Set heat to medium-high for it to boil, it should take about 5 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, cut the eggplants crosswise into 2.5 inch pieces. Cut the thicker pieces in half lengthwise
  3. When the water starts boiling, put the prepared eggplants on the steamer rack and cover. Steam for 5 minutes over medium high heat.
  4. Remove from the heat. Take out the steamed eggplant and transfer to a cutting board. Let cool for 5 to 10 minutes until you can easily handle them.
  5. Tear each piece into bite size pieces.
  6. Combine garlic, green onion, soy sauce, fish sauce, hot pepper flakes, and sesame oil in a mixing bowl
  7. Mix the ingredients well with a wide spoon
  8. Add the steamed eggplant to the mixing bowl and mix well, coating the eggplant pieces.
  9. Top with crushed sesame seeds and serve with rice.
